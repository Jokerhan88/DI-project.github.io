  • Introduction

    CI Labs and Studios run throughout the year and are open to anyone working in Edinburgh’s creative and cultural community. Events are free to attend and offer an excellent introduction to the wider Creative Informatics programme.

    • Sound and Vision

    We are delighted to welcome dance artist Lisa May Thomas as our first CI Labs featured artist. Lisa is a contemporary dance artist, based in Bristol, who has worked extensively with dance for screen and is currently developing live choreographic, sensorial performances with new technologies. Lisa will run a VR demonstration throughout the evening, giving participants an insight in to how VR can be applied in an artistic setting. You can find out more about Lisa (@lisamaythomas) and her work at: https://vimeo.com/lisamaythomas / http://www.figuring-project.com/

  • Gender Distribution

    In terms of gender, there is not much difference between the number of male participants and female participants. From the data, male participants appear more in CI Studios and female take a larger proportion in CI Labs.

  • Age Distribution

    Middle-aged people are the main participants in CI events. But sometimes it will be affected by the theme of the event, for example, young people are more interested in interesting data display and technology, while senior people are not so keen on immersive experience.
